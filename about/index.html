About


Born in 1988, a student of the Fine Arts University of Cluj a cultural hub of Romania, Eugen Roșca discovers after graduation, the Paintbrush Factory which was in it’s early stages of inception. There, he works as an assistant for Sabot gallery for a few months. After a brief encounter with Mihai Pop, cofounder of Plan B Gallery, transitions and starts working in close collaboration with him in art production and helping with organizing the gallerie’s exhibitions. For a period of a few years Roșca develops o keen sense of what contemporary art is and has the opportunity of meeting and spending some time with the gallerie’s artists. Among others, Ciprian Mureșan, Șerban Savu and Adrian Ghenie.

In 2013, he rents a small studio in the Paintbrush Factory and, inspired by the old masters of the renaissance starts painting in tempera on wood as a first choice. After 6 months, first works start to emerge and he gets visited by Mihai Pop who is pleased of what he sees. As a crucial pivot in time he gets the offer of exhibiting with Plan B Berlin in 2014. The show it’s called “Knife Seller” and it features mostly oil on wood and tempera paintings.

After three years, Suzana Vasilescu director of Suprainfinit Gallery, offers Roșca a collaboration for a solo show. “Shallow And Profound” takes place. After two more years, another show. This time titled “Recent Works”.

Generally, Roșca's works draws element inspiration from many cultures. Among others it has something to do with magical realism. At the core of the paintings is the depiction of the unconscious mind. He enjoys resolving the previously contradictory conditions of dream and realty into an absolute reality, a super-reality and features the factor of surprise, unexpected juxtapositions and non sequitur.

As a recent development Roșca also begun working with text.
